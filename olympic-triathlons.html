Olympic Triathlons

Standard Olympic Triathlon Distance

  • Swim: .9 miles
  • Bike: 26 miles
  • Run: 6.2 miles

The Olympic triathlon was declared an official triathlon distance of the Sydney Olympic Games in 2000. It's a competitive endurance sport that's designed spectator-friendly; usually, the course loops around to accommodate the audience.

While it has a reputation for being ultra-competitive, the “olympic tri” is also great for novice triathletes because it is less grueling than Ironman distances. To properly train for an olympic triathlon, you should plan to start training 12 weeks before race day.

Training for an Olympic Triathlon

While the race is shorter than the Ironman, the olympic tri's distance shouldn't be underestimated. To properly prepare for this long-distance race, you'll need to do periodization training - a method in which you train in different phases that emphasize and improve endurance and then speed.

Selecting a Race

The USA Triathlon website is an excellent resource for picking out a nearby race. To get the most out of your training, try to choose a race that is about 18 weeks away.

Nutrition

Make sure you include nutrition in your triathlon training and racing. This sport is more than just exceptional athleticism and fitness: it is also about following a specially designed, strict diet. Read through some of our triathlon articles for more in-depth information on nutrition for triathletes.

Tools of Trade

In order to complete a triathlon, you only need a few accessories: a swim cap, road bicycle and helmet, and running shoes. Gear for beginners tends to emphasize more support, while gear for advanced triathletes tends to emphasize less weight in order to shave off time.
